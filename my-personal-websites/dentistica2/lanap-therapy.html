LANAP Therapy

LANAP (Laser-Assisted New Attachment Procedure) is a minimally invasive periodontal protocol using a specialised dental laser to remove diseased tissue, reduce bacteria and promote wound healing and tissue reattachment — often avoiding more extensive flap surgery. At Dentistica we combine evidence-based protocols with laser expertise for predictable periodontal regeneration when indicated.

When LANAP is considered
  • Advanced periodontal pockets with bone loss where regenerative treatment is an option
  • Areas not responding to conventional non-surgical therapy
  • Patients seeking less invasive alternatives to traditional surgery

How LANAP works at Dentistica

Assess → laser decontamination → healing & maintenance

LANAP uses a specific periodontal laser wavelength to selectively remove diseased pocket epithelium and bacteria while preserving healthy tissue. The protocol encourages a stable clot and potential new attachment of soft tissue and bone. We perform the procedure under local anaesthesia with careful post-op monitoring and maintenance therapy.

1 — Comprehensive assessment

Pocket charting, CBCT/radiographs where needed, and discussion of clinical expectations and alternatives.

2 — LANAP protocol

Laser decontamination of the pocket, minimal mechanical debridement, clot stabilization and instructions for optimal healing.

3 — Re-evaluation & maintenance

Follow-up visits to monitor pocket depth changes and place the patient on a periodontal maintenance schedule to maintain results.

Benefits
  • Often less post-op discomfort and bleeding vs conventional surgery
  • Preserves soft tissue architecture and may encourage regeneration
  • Can be combined with other regenerative measures when appropriate
Is LANAP suitable for everyone with periodontitis?

Not always — candidacy depends on pocket depth, bone defects, overall health and smoking status. We will assess and recommend the best personalised approach.

What is recovery like?

Most patients experience mild soreness and require 1–2 weeks of gentle care. We provide post-op instructions and pain management recommendations.
